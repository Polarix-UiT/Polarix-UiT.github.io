SERPENT

SEPARATION RESEARCH PROJECT NARVIK UIT

Serpent is a student-led initiative aimed at designing and building a 2U CubeSat prototype for a suborbital flight. SERPENT laid the groundwork for Polarix organisation and started this adventure.

Project Overview

Mission

Design and build a 2U CubeSat for a suborbital flight, capable of separating into 2 1U satellites after launch and deployment.

Low level focus

Our focus is to apply engineering skills taught at UiT The Arctic University of Norway to develop space-grade hardware from the ground up.

Collaboration

SERPENT was developed in collaboration with Andøya Space and UiT The Arctic University of Norway, which provided guidance, testing facilities, and access to their expertise in satellite technology.

Mission Objectives

Detumbling

Design and build an ADCS (Attitude Determination and Control System) capable of detumbling the CubeSat during a suborbital flight.

Separation

Design and build a separation mechanism to separate the CubeSat into 2 1U satellites after launch and deployment.

Separation verification

Design and build a sensor subsystem to verify the successful separation of the CubeSat.

Project Timeline

May 2024

Intent to Fly

An Intent to Fly was submitted to Andøya Space to participate in the GHOST mission.

August 2024

Project Kick-off

Project officially commenced with initial team meetings. Students were recruited and divided into teams.

January 2025

Critical Design Review

The design was reviewed and finalized.

July 2025

Payload Integration

The payload was tested and integrated into the rocket at Wallops.

November 2025

Launch

Scheduled launch date from Andøya Space.
